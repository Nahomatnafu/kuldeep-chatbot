## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure defines the mandatory Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) protocol for all maintenance and servicing activities on equipment at Facility 4B. LOTO procedures protect personnel from unexpected energization, startup, or release of stored energy that could cause injury or death.

This procedure complies with **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147** — *The Control of Hazardous Energy*.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to all employees, contractors, and temporary workers who perform maintenance, cleaning, unjamming, adjusting, or inspection activities on machinery where unexpected startup is possible. This includes but is not limited to:

- Conveyors and belt systems
- Hydraulic and pneumatic press machines
- CNC machining centers
- Robotic welding cells
- Automated guided vehicles (AGVs)

---

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Authorized Employee** | Person who performs LOTO and services the equipment |
| **Affected Employee** | Person who operates the machine under normal conditions |
| **Energy Isolating Device** | A mechanical device that physically prevents transmission of energy (e.g., circuit breaker, valve) |
| **Lockout Device** | A lock used to hold an energy isolating device in the safe position |
| **Tagout Device** | A warning tag affixed to an energy isolating device to warn against re-energization |
| **Zero Energy State** | Condition in which all hazardous energy is isolated and residual energy is released/restrained |

---

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Authorized Employees (Maintenance Technicians)
- Complete LOTO training annually (minimum).
- Apply personal locks and complete all steps in Section 6 before servicing.
- Never lend or share personal locks with others.

### 4.2 Affected Employees (Operators)
- Notify supervisor when equipment is taken out of service.
- Never attempt to restart or operate equipment bearing a LOTO device.
- Report any missing, damaged, or bypassed LOTO devices to Maintenance immediately.

### 4.3 Shift Lead / Supervisor
- Verify LOTO is applied before authorizing any maintenance activity.
- Maintain the LOTO Log (Form SF-030) in the production office.

---

## 5. Required Equipment

- Personal padlocks (keyed differently; issued individually — see Tool Crib)
- Lockout hasps (multi-lock, accommodates up to 6 locks)
- Danger tags ("DO NOT OPERATE" — red, Form SF-031)
- Lockout station kit: includes valve lockouts, circuit breaker lockouts, plug lockouts
- Safety chain (for large valve handles)

## 6. LOTO Procedure — Step by Step

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do not skip or reorder steps. Each step is critical to safety.

### Step 1 — Notify Affected Employees
Inform all operators and affected personnel that the equipment is being taken out of service for maintenance. Specify:
- Equipment name and ID
- Estimated duration
- Shutdown method

### Step 2 — Identify All Energy Sources
Consult the machine-specific **Energy Control Procedure (ECP)** card posted on the machine. Identify ALL energy sources:
- [ ] Electrical (main disconnect, control circuits)
- [ ] Pneumatic (compressed air supply lines)
- [ ] Hydraulic (pump and accumulator circuits)
- [ ] Mechanical (stored energy: springs, gravity loads)
- [ ] Thermal (steam lines, process heat)

### Step 3 — Shut Down the Equipment
Using the normal stopping procedure for the machine, bring it to a complete stop. Use the machine's operator interface to initiate controlled shutdown.

### Step 4 — Isolate All Energy Sources
Operate each energy-isolating device to the OFF or CLOSED position. Follow the ECP card for the sequence. Do not rely on memory — use the card.

### Step 5 — Apply Lockout Devices
Attach your **personal padlock** to each energy-isolating device. If multiple devices share a single lockout point, use a **hasp** so all technicians can apply individual locks.

- Attach a **Danger Tag** (Form SF-031) to each lock.
- Fill in: your name, date, time, and reason for LOTO.

### Step 6 — Release/Restrain Stored Energy
Dissipate or restrain all residual energy before touching the equipment:

| Energy Type | Release Method |
|-------------|---------------|
| Electrical | Verify voltage with a meter; test for live circuits |
| Pneumatic | Open bleed valve; verify gauge reads 0 PSI |
| Hydraulic | Cycle controls with power off; verify gauge reads 0 PSI |
| Springs | Block or release spring tension using fixtures |
| Gravity loads | Block with mechanical supports; do not trust hydraulics |
| Capacitors | Allow 5-minute discharge period; verify with meter |

### Step 7 — Verify Zero Energy State (LOTO Test)
Before performing any work, **attempt to start the machine** using normal controls. This is the critical verification step.

- Push START button or activate controls.
- If the machine starts or moves → **STOP IMMEDIATELY**. Return to Step 4.
- If nothing happens → Zero Energy State is confirmed. Proceed with work.

> ✅ **Document the LOTO application in Form SF-030 before beginning work.**

## 7. Restoring Equipment to Service (LOTO Removal)

Complete work must be fully finished before removing LOTO. Follow steps in order:

1. **Remove all tools**, materials, and personnel from the equipment and danger zone.
2. **Reinstall all guards** and protective devices removed during maintenance.
3. **Notify affected employees** that equipment is being returned to service.
4. **Remove your personal lock and tag** — only the person who applied them may remove them.
5. **Re-energize** the equipment following the normal startup procedure.
6. **Record LOTO removal** in Form SF-030: date, time, your signature.

---

## 8. Group LOTO (Multi-Person Maintenance)

When more than one person works on the same equipment simultaneously:

1. The Lead Technician applies the primary lock to all energy isolating devices.
2. A **group lockout box** is used — the Lead Technician's key is placed in the box, which is then locked.
3. **Each additional worker applies their own personal lock** to the group box.
4. Each worker removes their own lock upon completing their portion of work.
5. The Lead Technician removes the group box lock last, after all individual locks are removed.

---

## 9. Shift Change During LOTO

If a LOTO must remain active across a shift change:

1. Outgoing tech and incoming tech both sign the LOTO Log (Form SF-030).
2. Incoming tech applies their lock **before** outgoing tech removes theirs (continuity of lockout).
3. Shift Lead must verify transfer is documented.

---

## 10. Emergency LOTO Removal

If a worker has left the facility with their lock applied, the following procedure must be followed by the Shift Lead/Supervisor:

1. Confirm the worker is not present on-site and cannot be contacted.
2. Verify that the equipment can be safely re-energized.
3. Use the master key (located in Plant Manager's office — requires dual authorization).
4. Document the emergency removal in Form SF-030, noting reason and authorizing signatures.

> ⚠️ **This procedure must NEVER be used to bypass a valid LOTO.** Misuse is a terminable offense and OSHA violation.
