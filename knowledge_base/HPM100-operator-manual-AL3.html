1. Safety Information

⛔ DANGER — CRUSH HAZARD The hydraulic press ram exerts up to 100 tons of force. Never place hands, fingers, or any body part within the die area when the machine is powered. Death or permanent injury can result.
⚠ WARNING — HIGH PRESSURE HYDRAULICS The hydraulic system operates at up to 3,000 PSI. Hydraulic fluid injection injuries are life-threatening. Never loosen fittings or open the system while pressurized. Always follow LOTO procedure SOP-002 and verify accumulator is fully bled (0 PSI) before any maintenance.
⚠ WARNING — PINCH POINTS Keep fingers clear of all guide rails, slide ways, and moving components during operation. Never bypass or defeat safety guarding.
ℹ NOTE — LOTO REQUIREMENT All maintenance, die changes, unjamming, and cleaning inside the press area require a full Lockout/Tagout per SOP-002. The hydraulic accumulator retains stored energy after shutdown — it must be manually bled before work begins.

1.1 Required PPE

  • Safety glasses (ANSI Z87.1)
  • Steel-toed boots (ASTM F2413)
  • Hearing protection (when running in cycle mode — 86 dB area)
  • Cut-resistant gloves (die handling only)
  • Face shield (when checking hydraulic fluid or bleeding system)

2. Equipment Overview

The Greenerd HPM-100 is a 100-ton hydraulic stamping press used at Station 5 on Assembly Line 3 to form and stamp brake housing assemblies (Part No. BRK-ASSY-7710). The press uses a double-acting hydraulic cylinder to provide controlled pressing force. An onboard PLC manages stroke, dwell time, and force monitoring. The machine is equipped with light curtains and a two-hand control for operator safety.

5. Startup Procedure

ℹ PRE-REQUISITE Startup must not begin until the AL-3 startup checklist (SOP-001) has been completed and signed. Verify no LOTO devices are applied to this machine.
1 Confirm all guards are installed and the light curtain is unobstructed. Verify the die set is correct for the current work order — check Part No. against the production traveler.
2 Check hydraulic fluid level in the sight glass on the reservoir. Level must be between the MIN and MAX marks. If below MIN, add ISO VG 46 oil using the fill port on the top of the tank.
3 Confirm main electrical disconnect (MCP-AL3, CB-22) is in the ON position.
4 Turn the MODE SELECT key switch to SINGLE CYCLE.
5 Press the green POWER ON button. The hydraulic pump will start. Allow 2 minutes for the system to reach operating temperature before running production.
6 Verify the CYCLE READY green LED illuminates. If FAULT LED is lit, refer to Section 8 before proceeding.
7 Run 3 setup strokes with no part (air strokes). Observe ram for smooth, consistent travel. Check for hydraulic leaks around the cylinder and hose connections.
8 Run 3 units for First Article Inspection per QP-017. Do not run production until QI signs off Form QC-104.

6. Operating Procedure — Single Cycle Mode

  1. Place the component in the die set, ensuring correct seating against all locating pins.
  2. Verify both hands are on the two-hand palm buttons and no part of your body is within the light curtain zone.
  3. Press and hold both CYCLE START buttons simultaneously. The press will complete a full stroke and automatically retract.
  4. When the CYCLE READY LED illuminates, release the palm buttons and remove the completed part.
  5. Inspect the first part each hour against the in-process inspection criteria on Form QC-104.
  6. If the TONNAGE display reads outside the programmed limits (displayed as HIGH or LOW alarm), stop production and notify the Shift Lead immediately.
⚠ NEVER reach into the die area to retrieve a stuck part while the machine is powered. Apply LOTO per SOP-002 before removing a jammed part.

7. Shutdown Procedure

  1. Complete the current production run. Ensure the ram is in the fully retracted (home) position.
  2. Remove the last part from the die and place in the completed parts bin.
  3. Press the red E-STOP button.
  4. Turn the MODE SELECT key to the locked position and remove the key. Give key to the Shift Lead.
  5. Clean the die area of metal chips and debris using a brush — do not use compressed air on the die.
  6. Record end-of-shift production count, cycle count, and any issues in the Shift Log (Form PRD-010).

9. Daily Maintenance (Operator Responsibility)

  • Check hydraulic fluid level — between MIN and MAX on sight glass.
  • Inspect external surfaces of cylinder, hoses, and fittings for leaks. Report any leaks to Maintenance.
  • Verify light curtain function — interrupt the curtain and confirm the machine does not cycle.
  • Test one E-STOP button per shift (rotate which one). Confirm machine stops and FAULT LED lights.
  • Clean die area of metal chips, slugs, and debris after each production run.
  • Record completed maintenance in the Daily Log (Form QC-104, Section B).

10. Emergency Procedures

10.1 Part Jammed in Die

  1. Press E-STOP immediately.
  2. Apply full LOTO per SOP-002. Bleed hydraulic accumulator — verify 0 PSI on gauge.
  3. Use die removal tools (die cart in aisle, die bars at Station 5 tool board) to remove the part. Never use hands.
  4. Inspect die for damage before returning to production.
  5. Record the jam event in Form QC-201 (Non-Conformance Report).

10.2 Hydraulic Fluid Spill

  1. Press E-STOP immediately.
  2. Use absorbent pads from the spill kit (Station 5 wall cabinet) to contain the spill.
  3. Do not allow fluid to reach drains. Use drain barriers if necessary.
  4. Call Maintenance (ext. 4112) and EHS (ext. 4200).
  5. Document spill in the EHS Spill Log (Form EHS-015).
