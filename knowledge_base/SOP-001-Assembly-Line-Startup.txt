1. PURPOSE
----------
This SOP defines the standard startup procedure for Assembly Line 3 (AL-3) at
Facility 4B. It ensures that all equipment, safety systems, and personnel are
properly prepared before production begins. Following this procedure eliminates
unplanned downtime, reduces defect rates, and maintains compliance with ISO
9001:2015 and OSHA 29 CFR 1910 standards.

2. SCOPE
--------
This procedure applies to all operators, leads, and supervisors assigned to
AL-3 during first-shift, second-shift, and weekend production runs. It must
be completed in full before the first unit enters the line.

3. RESPONSIBILITIES
-------------------
- Line Operator:       Perform all checklist steps. Sign the daily log.
- Shift Lead:          Verify completion. Counter-sign the daily log.
- Maintenance Tech:    Respond within 15 minutes to any flagged equipment
                       issue before production is authorized.
- Quality Inspector:   Conduct first-article inspection after startup.

4. REQUIRED PPE
---------------
All personnel must be wearing the following before entering AL-3:
  [ ] Safety glasses (ANSI Z87.1 rated)
  [ ] Steel-toed boots (ASTM F2413)
  [ ] High-visibility vest (Class 2 minimum)
  [ ] Cut-resistant gloves when handling sheet metal components

5. EQUIPMENT & MATERIALS NEEDED
---------------------------------
  - AL-3 Daily Startup Log (Form QC-104)
  - Digital torque wrench (calibration current — see tag on handle)
  - Calibrated pressure gauge (0–200 PSI range)
  - Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) kit — assigned to operator
  - Lubricant: Mobilux EP2 grease (bin location: Rack B-12)
  - Cleaning rags (lint-free, Part No. MNT-CLN-001)

6. PROCEDURE
------------

STEP 1: FACILITY & SAFETY WALKTHROUGH (Est. 10 min)
  1.1  Inspect AL-3 aisle for trip hazards, spills, and obstructions.
       Clear any debris to designated waste bins.
  1.2  Verify that all emergency stop (E-STOP) buttons are unobstructed
       and visually accessible. Test one E-STOP per shift per the test
       log — do NOT test all; rotate which one is tested.
  1.3  Confirm fire extinguisher at Station 7 is in the green zone
       and last inspection date is within 12 months.
  1.4  Check that first-aid kit at Station 1 is stocked. Flag missing
       items to the shift lead immediately.

STEP 2: CONVEYANCE SYSTEM INSPECTION (Est. 15 min)
  2.1  Inspect conveyor belt (Sections A–F) for visible tears, fraying,
       or misalignment. Do not start line if belt damage is observed;
       contact Maintenance (ext. 4112).
  2.2  Verify belt tension using the tension gauge at the tail pulley.
       Acceptable range: 80–110 lbs. Record reading in Form QC-104.
  2.3  Lubricate all conveyor bearing points (12 total) with Mobilux EP2.
       Apply 3–5 pumps per fitting. Wipe excess grease.
  2.4  Inspect all guide rails for straightness. Adjust alignment brackets
       if lateral deviation exceeds 2mm. See Work Instruction WI-014.

STEP 3: PNEUMATIC SYSTEM CHECK (Est. 5 min)
  3.1  Open the main pneumatic supply valve (yellow handle, Panel P-3).
  3.2  Allow system pressure to stabilize for 60 seconds.
  3.3  Verify system pressure reads 90–110 PSI on the main gauge.
  3.4  Inspect air hoses at Stations 2, 5, and 9 for cracks or leaks.
       Apply soapy water to fittings if a leak is suspected.
  3.5  Drain condensate from the air dryer drain valve. Close fully.

STEP 4: TOOLING & FIXTURE VERIFICATION (Est. 10 min)
  4.1  Confirm that correct tooling revision is installed per the current
       production traveler. Check revision letter against Tool Matrix TM-22.
  4.2  Inspect all fixtures for damage, missing locating pins, or loose
       clamps. Replace or tighten as needed before proceeding.
  4.3  Perform a torque audit on five (5) randomly selected fixture bolts
       using the calibrated torque wrench. Required torque: 25 ± 2 Nm.
       Record results in Form QC-104, Section C.

STEP 5: ELECTRICAL & CONTROLS SYSTEM (Est. 5 min)
  5.1  Power on the main control panel (MCP-AL3). Wait for PLC HMI to
       fully boot (approx. 45 seconds).
  5.2  Acknowledge any latched alarms from the previous shift. Investigate
       any FAULT alarms with Maintenance before authorizing startup.
  5.3  Confirm machine vision system (Camera C1 and C2) display live feed.
       Clean camera lenses if image appears hazy — use lens wipe only.
  5.4  Run conveyor at 20% speed for 2 minutes. Observe for unusual noise
       or vibration. Log observations in Form QC-104.

STEP 6: FIRST ARTICLE INSPECTION (Est. 15 min)
  6.1  Run 3 units through the full assembly process.
  6.2  Hand off units to Quality Inspector for FAI per QP-017.
  6.3  DO NOT begin production run until Quality Inspector signs off FAI
       on Form QC-104, Section D.

7. SIGN-OFF & DOCUMENTATION
----------------------------
  [ ] Line Operator prints name, signs, and records start time on Form QC-104.
  [ ] Shift Lead reviews and counter-signs Form QC-104.
  [ ] Completed form is submitted to the Production Office by 08:30 daily.

8. DEVIATIONS & ESCALATION
---------------------------
Any failure to meet specification during startup (e.g., pressure out of range,
failed FAI, equipment damage) must be reported to the Shift Lead immediately.
Production must NOT begin until the deviation is resolved and documented on
Non-Conformance Report NCR Form (Form QC-201).
